    This is an open-book exam. You may not:

    • Communicate (by any means) with anyone but me

    You may:

    • Refer to any books you want
    • Refer to any notes you want
    • Refer to any files you want (as long as you're not communicating with anyone)
    • Visit any site you want on the World Wide Web (as long as you're not communicating with anyone)
    • Use any software installed on your computer
    • Ask me questions

    Attribution: If you use someone else's work (for example, a Web site) in one of your answers, you must give credit. The only exceptions are that you do not need to give credit for ideas you get from me or from the textbook.

    Pledging: There is, of course, nowhere to sign an electronic exam. By submitting work on this exam, therefore, you are pledging that your work has been done in accordance with the Honor Code.

    Clarity counts. If I can figure out what your answers mean without guessing, that's good for credit. If I have to guess, I will take off credit accordingly.

    Download midterm-work.html to some private space you control. (I don't care where, as long as the space isn't shared with anybody else.) Answer the following questions by editing that file. When you are done, turn in your work by attaching your edited file to the last question.

    Whether you put anything in the text blanks for the questions is up to you.  I plan to grade you on the file you attach, not on the text (if any) in the blanks, although by putting HTML/CSS in the blanks you might be able to provide yourself with a hedge against any failure in the file attachment process.
