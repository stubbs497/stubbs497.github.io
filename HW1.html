Halloween

  • Halloween is a holiday that takes place on October 31.
  • This holiday is usually celebrated by people dressing up and asking for candy.
  • Originally it was a Pagan traditon called Samhain and was adapted into what it is today.

To Get Ready For Halloween

  1. Decorate your house
  2. Buy candy and set it out
  3. Be ready to answer the door

Halloween Superstitions

Black Cats

Black Cats are seen as a sign of bad luck.
They are believed to messengers to the devil and the compainions of witches.

Jack-O'-Lanterns

Jack-O'-Latners are used to guide losts souls.
Originally they were made out of turnips but then became pumpkins.
The scary faces carved onto the pumpkins were used to scare evil spirits away.

Bats

Bats like black cats are seen as a sign of bad luck.
Their relationship to witches is that bats are their famillers.

Witches

The idea of witches stems from the pagan goddess known as The Crone.
The Crone was worshiped on Samhain.

Trick-Or-Treating

Trick-Or-Treating comes from the idea that ghosts would visit the living.
They would disguise themselves, knock on doors asking for money or food.
